Context
Why This Project

Three years into a mech eng degree without designing a complete electromechanical system or touching CATIA. Built the kind of actuator used in Optimus and modern legged robots to close that gap.

Design Philosophy

ApproachRequirements before CAD, trade studies before detail design
ArchitectureSame QDD used in Optimus, ANYmal, modern legged robots
PriorityTransparency: backdrivable by hand, low friction
CollaborationCo-run with a previous Tesla Optimus intern
Key insight The #1 failure mode in student robotics is not the gearbox, but how it attaches to the motor. Interface definition was prioritized from day one.

Single skeleton drives all parts. Change one parameter, whole assembly updates.

Core Principles

ConstraintsParts constrain to skeleton, never to each other
ReferencesNo B-Rep refs. Sketch on planes, reference wireframe
PositioningChain-of-zeros: skeleton at origin, parts relative to sub-assembly
Cutting bodiesBearing pockets + bolt holes reference skeleton datums only
PatterningBody-level over feature-level for robustness

No CATIA experience before this project. Most of the 60-80 hours went into figuring out what not to do. Placeholder gears act as geometric contracts between sub-assemblies.
