4th Year Mechanical Engineering · University of Victoria

I like building things that move. Most of my engineering time goes into actuator design, mechanism kinematics, and figuring out how to make 3D-printed parts do things they probably shouldn't.

Right now I'm designing a quasi-direct-drive gearbox from scratch: trade studies, parametric CATIA, FDM tolerancing, and structured prototype testing. It's the kind of project where most of the progress comes from finding out what doesn't work.

Outside of engineering I spend most of my time mountain biking, fishing, and exploring Vancouver Island. The hitch project on this site started because I needed a cheap way to carry bikes on logging roads without destroying them.

I'm looking for internship opportunities in actuator and mechanism design. If you're working on something interesting, I'd like to hear about it.
