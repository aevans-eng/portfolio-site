Cut inside the deferral lines

In November 2021 the province's Old Growth Technical Advisory Panel mapped British Columbia's most at-risk old growth and recommended deferring it from logging. Around Port Renfrew, recorded harvest overlaps those recommended-deferral areas by about 138 hectares. Most of that harvest began before the recommendation existed. Nine openings did not.

The nine below have a recorded harvest start date after November 2, 2021, inside areas the panel had already flagged, totalling about 30.2 hectares. Several sit in forest the province's own mapping classes as ancient, older than 400 years, concentrated around Walbran Creek, the Caycuse River, and the Loss landscape unit.

A deferral recommendation is not a law, and these areas were not all formally deferred, so harvest here is not proof any rule was broken. What does not exist is a public record of why these blocks went ahead: the site plans, the old-growth retention rationale, and the sale economics are not published. That is the finding. Each opening ID is checkable in the province's own RESULTS system, and what you can do covers how to ask for the records behind any of them.
