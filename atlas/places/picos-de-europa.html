The hook

A wall of sheer grey limestone rising a few miles inland from the wet northern coast, close enough to the sea that sailors used it to know they were nearly home.

01Why it's worth it

The Picos are a compact, brutal little range, all vertical rock and deep gorges packed into a small area between Asturias and Cantabria. Because the Atlantic sits right there, the weather is fierce and the greenery below the crags is Irish, which is why cider and blue cheese come from these valleys rather than anything Mediterranean. The set pieces are easy to reach: the Fuente Dé cable car lifts you most of a kilometre up a cliff face in a few minutes to a high stony world of ibex and hikers; the Cares Gorge trail cuts a ledge path through a canyon between two villages; and the Covadonga lakes sit in a glacial bowl above the shrine where the Reconquest is said to have started. You can taste all of it from a car and a lift, or disappear into it for days.

02How to experience it

Ride the Fuente Dé cable car early before the cloud comes up the valley, then walk out along the top for as long as your legs want. The Ruta del Cares is the classic day walk, a broad cliff ledge that is spectacular and popular; start early from the Poncebos end. Base in a valley village for the cheese and cider.
