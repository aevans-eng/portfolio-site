The hook

A one-square-mile village of storybook cottages with no street numbers and a white-sand beach at the bottom of the main street.

01Why it's worth it

Carmel is precious and it knows it, so set expectations: the main drag can feel like an upscale outdoor mall in peak season. Two blocks off it, though, it turns into a genuine oddity, a town that banned street addresses and streetlights and filled itself with fairy-tale cottages, many built by one 1920s designer who seemed allergic to right angles. Ocean Avenue runs straight downhill to a wide, soft, free, dog-friendly beach that's better than any of the shopping. Come for the residential lanes and the sand; treat the galleries as scenery.

02How to experience it

Park up the hill and walk down Ocean Avenue to the beach, then lose the crowd in the residential blocks north and south, hunting the Comstock storybook cottages tucked among the trees.

03Skip the cliché

Don't spend the whole visit on the boutique-and-gallery strip. The cottage lanes and the beach are the real Carmel, and they cost nothing.

From the locals

It shows up as a genuine stop, not a trophy: people pair "Carmel by the sea" with Point Lobos and rave about small things like the nitro cold brew at Mad Dogs and Englishmen, which is the register of a place people actually use.
